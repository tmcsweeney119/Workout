Tim · New Orleans · 2025
Progressive Overload
Add 2–5 lbs when you hit the top of a rep range for 2 consecutive sessions with clean form. Never chase weight at the cost of technique. Your muscle memory will return faster than you expect — the strength is still in there.
Flexibility Is the Program
The pre-workout stretches, warm-up, and full cooldown are mandatory. They are not supplemental — they are half the workout. Skipping them is exactly where returning athletes get hurt and lose progress.
The Golf Transfer
Thoracic rotation, hip internal rotation, and lat length directly determine swing arc and clubhead speed. The flexibility sessions in this program will show up on the course before the strength does. Prioritize them accordingly.
Reps in Reserve (RIR)
Phase 1: stop 3–4 reps before failure every set. Phase 2: 2–3 reps early. Phase 3: 1–2 reps early on strength sets, closer to failure on hypertrophy days. RIR protects your connective tissue while it adapts — it is not weakness, it is strategy.
Neck — Every Single Day
The chin tuck (cervical retraction) takes 2 minutes and should be done every day including rest days. It is the single highest-return flexibility habit in this program. Forward head posture from desk work compresses the cervical spine and limits your backswing — fix it daily.
Sleep & Nutrition
Target 0.7–1g of protein per lb of bodyweight daily. Treat 8 hours of sleep as a non-negotiable part of the program — muscle, tendon, and connective tissue repair happens at rest, not in the gym. Do not aggressively cut calories while running this program simultaneously.
Joint Pain vs. Soreness
Muscle soreness (DOMS) in weeks 1–2 is expected and a good sign. Sharp or persistent pain in a joint — knee, shoulder, elbow, or lower back — is a different signal entirely. Rest that day and reassess before returning. One missed session costs nothing. An injury costs weeks.
